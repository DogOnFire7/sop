## 4.1 Pushback requirements
When pushing back aircraft, controllers should ensure that the pushing aircraft does not impede other pushing or taxiing aircraft. Simultaneous pushbacks should only be approved if there is a minimum spacing of two stands between the two aircraft.


## 4.2 South GA Apron
Aircraft generally do not require pushback. Startup clearance should be issued, with the instruction “Report when ready for taxi”.


## 4.3 Main Apron
Aircraft require pushback from the main apron. Aircraft should be instructed to pushback facing either North or South, generally in the direction facing their departure holding point, or which provides the shortest taxi route, as long as it does not impede taxiing traffic.

## 4.4 North Apron
Aircraft generally do not require pushback. Startup clearance should be issued, with the instruction “Report when ready for taxi”.


## 4.5 North GA Apron
Aircraft generally do not require pushback. Startup clearance should be issued, with the instruction “Report when ready for taxi”.


## 4.6 East Apron
Aircraft require pushback from the east apron. Aircraft should be instructed to pushback facing South.


## 4.7 Seaplane terminal
Aircraft do not require pushback. Startup clearance should be issued with the readback correct message as described in section 1.3.3.


## 4.8 Phraseology
!!! headphone "Example pushback clearance"
    Sky Surfer 102 (DQA102) pushback and startup approved facing north

    “Pushback and startup approved facing north, Sky Surfer 102 (DQA102)”

!!! headphone "Example startup clearance"
    Sky Surfer 102 (DQA102) startup approved, report when ready for taxi

    “Startup approved, report when ready for taxi, Sky Surfer 102 (DQA102)”
