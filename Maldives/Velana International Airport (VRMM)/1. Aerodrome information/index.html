    1. Aerodrome information

    1.1 Aerodrome introduction

    Velana International Airport (VRMM) is the primary airport for the Maldives, located on Hulhulé Island, east of the capital, Male'. The aerodrome first opened in 1960, undergoing several developments and rennovations across the time it has been open, including a new runway which entered full operational service in 2022, and a new international terminal opening in 2025. Velena International handles effectively all of the nations international and domestic traffic, along with being home to the wolds largest seaplane airline and base.

    1.6 Prefered runway operation

    The runway in operation at Velena international should be decided based off the current weather / wind conditions. Between 36 and 18, the runway with the highest headwind should be used. If headwind in equal (zero), or if the runway can not be decided on, the prefered runway is 36.

    The runway(s) in operation at the seaplane base are dependent on the runway in operation at the international airport, as shown in the table below.

    International airport Seaplane base (standard) Seaplane base (high crosswind)
    36 NL for takeoff
    NC or SL for landing (based off wind direction)
    18 SC for takeoff
    SL for landing

    Useful tip

    We recomend controllers to check and use the runway(s) being operated in real life through sites such as FR24, ADSB Exchange, etc. If controllers think that the runway in operation should change, they can check what is being operated in the real world to help their decision.
